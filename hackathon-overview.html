Hackathon Overview

What is a Hackathon?

Hackathons are events where teams come together to collaboratively work on a creative and new project. Participants brainstorm ideas, come up with possible solutions to their problem, and design a working prototype by the end of the hackathon. Hackathons often have a specific theme, providing a focused direction for the projects.

Bootcamp Hackathon Details

The last two days of the bootcamp will have students working together in teams to design and build a project using what they learned from the bootcamp in a hackathon-style format. This year’s theme is Deploying AI in the Real World with AMD Technologies, reflecting the bootcamp’s focus on AI and Machine Learning.

In the hackathon, partipants will learn:

  • AI and ML Application: Apply and deepen understanding of artificial intelligence and machine learning through trainings and mentorship.
  • Collaborative Learning: Engage in teamwork to apply new skils effectively.
  • Project Management: Utilize strategies to plan, execute, and track project progress.
  • Prototype Development: Design and create a functional prototype.
  • Effective Presentation: Demonstrate a project effectively during the final presentation session.

Hackathon Rules

Here are the general guidelines for the hackathon.

  • Team size: 4 – 6 students per team​
  • Presentation time limit: 7 minutes
    • 5 minutes: presentation & demo​
    • 2 minutes: judge questions​
  • Presentation should contain:
    • Research Question / Idea Overview​
    • Relevance to theme​
    • Technologies used​
      • Models​
      • Peripherals​
    • Embrace teamwork and collaboration​
    • Process and Documentation of Work​
      • What went right (and wrong!)​
  • Demo
  • Have fun!

Hackathon Judging Criteria

These are what the judges will be grading each project on

  • Problem Statement & Usefulness
    • Relevance to theme: Deploying AI in the Real World with AMD Technologies
  • Execution and Creativity
    • Model & Peripheral Usage
  • Teamwork and Collaboration
  • Leadership
    • New Award: Tech Lead
    • Returning students
      • Review last year's trainings
      • Brainstorm hackathon ideas
    • Pitch ideas during team formation
  • Learning
  • Presentation

Tips for a Successful Presentation

  • Clearly define the goal of your project
  • What problem is your project trying to solve
  • How did your project solve the problem
  • What model(s) did your project use
  • How did each member of the team contribute to the project

Hackathon Showcase

The hackathon showcase will take place on the last day of the bootcamp and will be at the Summit Retreat at AMD's office. We invite parents to come and see what projects the students created during the hackathon. Some notes about the showcase:

  • Exhibition style: Each team will have a table in the summit retreat where they will set up their project and presentation. Parents and judges will be able to walk around and look at and ask questions about each project.
  • Teams will have 7 minutes to present their project to the judges during their assigned time slot.
  • Students, feel free to walk around and look at other projects when you are not presenting to the judges. Just make sure to always have at least three people at the station.
